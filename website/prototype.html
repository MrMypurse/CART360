PROJECT PROTOTYPE REPORT


HEAD IN THE CLOUDS

is an interative light installation. The light changes depending on the viewer's muscle movement.

- Written Response which addresses and relates the implicit concerns of Why Do We Prototype? & Fidelity Levels to the development process of your Physical Prototype

My initial idea of Head in the Clouds is an interactive sound and light wearable that is meant to be wear on the viewer's head. The light and the sound change depending on the viewer's head movement. I wanted the viewers to not only see and experience the project but become a part of the project. The viewers are not only observing the installation from a distance, but they have a chance to be completely inside the project. I intend for “Head in the Clouds” to be an immersive experience that makes people reflect on our perspective and the idea of being “inside” and “outside”. During my reflections, I thought about whether my project should offer the viewers an internal examination by cutting users off completely from the world and isolating them in a dark box, which takes them from a comfortable space to an uncomfortable space. On the contrary, my project can also explore the machinery of our own mind, which means that I can guide users through emotional state and to arrive at a more comfortable position. I wanted my project to seem welcoming, but it makes users question themselves once they are in that certain space. The project seems playful, but it isn’t, which might help the users realize something about themselves internally. After reflecting on these ideas, I decided that I want to attach the muscle sensor to the users, which makes them a part of the project (the installation wouldn’t work without their muscle movement). The LED lights will correspond to the movement of the muscle, so the users are essentially in control in a completely dark/unknown space. Therefore, the experience I want to convey is a transition of feeling unsure to feeling more at comfort/ in control. I have also been reflecting on the questions: why do we put this installation on? I think that I want the users to break out of their comfort zone by stepping into something unknown but that seems playful. And they will eventually gain the courage to observe more, realize that they are in control of this space. So overall, it should be an empowering experience which the users reflect on their fears/ overcome them.


- Technical Evaluation of Sensors and their associated Affordances which would ideally support your project’s proposed Interaction Design Strategy

 Arduino Uno: The microcontroller can read analogue EMG signals from the muscle sensor’s output. It is also used to control the LED light strips which changes according to the EMG signals.

LED light strips: The light changes according to the analogue EMG signals. When the muscle flexes/relaxes, the signal number goes up / down, which means that the LED lights will change its brightness and colors.

Myoware muscle sensor: It can be used to get EMG signals from our body. The output can be read using Arduino which can read analogue signals. As the muscle contracts or flexes the electrical activity increases and the analogue output of the Myoware sensor increases. As the muscle relaxers the electrical activity of the muscle decreases and the analogue output of the Myoware sensor decreases.


- Has your Project’s initial intention or supposed meaning changed over the course of researching and implementing the Physical Prototype? If YES or NO – Explain why?

Yes, my project’s initial intention has changed over the course of researching and implementing the physical prototype. In the early stage of my proposal process, I was unsure about the supposed meaning behind the project. I felt that there are many directions I can take. I am much surer about the intention of the project now that I have a physical prototype. Putting myself in an actual dark box made me realize how isolated the space can be and how uncomfortable that might make the users feel. I then thought about how users can use their body, more specifically, the EMG signals from their muscle, to take control of the space and to overcome their fears/anxiousness.
